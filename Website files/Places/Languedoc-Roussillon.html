Discover all that Languedoc-Roussillon has to offer!

What is there to do in Languedoc-Roussillon?

Languedoc-Roussillon, nestled in the southern part of France, is a region of diverse landscapes and rich cultural heritage. From the sandy beaches of the Mediterranean coast to the rolling vineyards and picturesque villages, Languedoc-Roussillon invites visitors to discover its unique charm. Explore historic cities, indulge in exquisite cuisine, and embark on outdoor adventures in this region where the past seamlessly blends with the present. Whether you're strolling through medieval streets or savoring local wines, Languedoc-Roussillon offers a captivating blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.

  • Discover Carcassonne's Medieval Citadel

    Step back in time as you explore the medieval citadel of Carcassonne, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Wander through narrow cobblestone streets, marvel at the well-preserved ramparts, and enter the imposing Château Comtal. The medieval charm of Carcassonne transports visitors to a bygone era, offering a glimpse into the region's storied history. Explore the Basilica of Saint-Nazaire and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding countryside from the city walls. Carcassonne's medieval citadel is a captivating destination for history enthusiasts and those seeking an immersive journey into the past.

  • Relax on the Beaches of Collioure

    Indulge in the coastal beauty of Collioure, a charming seaside town renowned for its vibrant colors and sandy beaches. Nestled between the Mediterranean Sea and the vine-covered hills, Collioure offers a perfect blend of relaxation and artistic inspiration. Spend leisurely days on the sandy shores, explore the historic Château Royal, and wander through the narrow streets adorned with art galleries. The unique light that attracted famous artists like Matisse and Derain still bathes Collioure in a magical glow, making it a haven for both beachgoers and art enthusiasts.

  • Explore the Pont du Gard

    Immerse yourself in ancient Roman engineering by visiting the Pont du Gard, an iconic aqueduct bridge that spans the Gardon River. This UNESCO World Heritage site stands as a testament to the ingenuity of Roman architecture, showcasing a well-preserved structure that once supplied water to the city of Nîmes. Marvel at the three-tiered arches, walk across the bridge, and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. The Pont du Gard is not only a historical marvel but also a picturesque destination where the ancient and natural worlds harmoniously converge.

  • Visit the Medieval Village of Minerve

    Step into the medieval charm of Minerve, a village perched on a rocky outcrop surrounded by deep gorges. Wander through narrow alleys, admire well-preserved medieval architecture, and visit the Cathar fortifications that bear witness to the region's tumultuous history. Explore the natural bridge of Minerve and take in the panoramic views of the rugged landscape. This historic village, designated as one of the "Les Plus Beaux Villages de France" (The Most Beautiful Villages of France), invites visitors to embark on a journey through time while surrounded by the scenic beauty of Languedoc-Roussillon.

  • Wine Tasting in the Vineyards of Saint-Chinian

    Indulge in the rich flavors of Languedoc-Roussillon by exploring the vineyards of Saint-Chinian. This renowned wine region is celebrated for its diverse and high-quality wines. Embark on a wine-tasting journey, visiting local wineries, and savoring the distinctive blends produced in the sun-drenched vineyards. Learn about the winemaking process, from grape cultivation to fermentation, and appreciate the passion and craftsmanship behind each bottle. The vineyards of Saint-Chinian offer a delightful experience for wine connoisseurs and enthusiasts seeking to discover the unique terroir of Languedoc-Roussillon.

Corsica, a Mediterranean gem situated between France and Italy, boasts rugged landscapes, including the impressive Corsican Alps and pristine beaches. This picturesque island, known for its unique blend of French and Italian influences, features charming hilltop villages and the historic birthplace of Napoleon Bonaparte in Ajaccio. With a rich cultural tapestry, Corsica offers a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, providing opportunities for hiking, water sports, and exploration of ancient ruins. It's a captivating destination that seamlessly blends natural beauty and cultural allure.

  • Explore Calanche de Piana

    Embark on a breathtaking adventure as you explore the captivating Calanche de Piana. This UNESCO World Heritage site boasts a stunning landscape of red granite cliffs and unique rock formations. Take a scenic drive or hike through this natural wonder, surrounded by the rugged beauty of Corsica. Marvel at the contrast of the deep blue Mediterranean sea against the vibrant red rocks, creating a visual spectacle that will leave a lasting impression. Calanche de Piana is not just a destination; it's a testament to the raw and untamed beauty that defines the Corsican landscape.

  • Relax on Palombaggia Beach

    Indulge in the paradise-like setting of Palombaggia Beach, renowned as one of Corsica's most beautiful beaches. With its fine white sand and crystal-clear turquoise waters, Palombaggia offers a perfect blend of relaxation and natural beauty. Spend your days lounging on the beach, taking refreshing dips in the sea, or exploring the nearby pine forests. The beach's idyllic setting, framed by pink granite rocks and lush vegetation, creates a serene and tranquil environment, making Palombaggia a must-visit destination for those seeking a blissful escape in the heart of the Mediterranean.

  • Discover the Citadel of Corte

    Immerse yourself in Corsica's rich history by exploring the Citadel of Corte. Perched on a rocky promontory, this historic fortress offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Wander through the cobblestone streets, visit the Museum of Corsica housed within the citadel, and soak in the medieval charm of this ancient stronghold. The Citadel of Corte not only provides a glimpse into Corsica's past but also serves as a symbolic focal point for the island's cultural heritage, making it a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts and those seeking a unique blend of architecture and history.

  • Explore the Scandola Nature Reserve

    Embark on a maritime adventure by exploring the pristine Scandola Nature Reserve. Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage site, Scandola is a sanctuary of biodiversity with its dramatic cliffs, hidden coves, and crystal-clear waters. Take a boat tour to witness the diverse marine life, including dolphins and seals, and marvel at the striking red cliffs that rise dramatically from the sea. Whether you're a nature enthusiast, a bird-watching aficionado, or simply someone seeking tranquility in a stunning natural setting, the Scandola Nature Reserve promises an unforgettable experience in the heart of Corsica's rugged coastline.

  • Hike the GR20 Trail

    Challenge yourself with a trek along the legendary GR20, often considered one of Europe's most challenging long-distance hiking trails. Traverse Corsica from north to south, passing through diverse landscapes, including lush forests, rugged mountains, and alpine lakes. The GR20 offers a thrilling adventure for hiking enthusiasts, providing both physical and mental rewards. Along the way, encounter charming mountain refuges, breathtaking viewpoints, and the warm hospitality of fellow hikers. Whether you embark on a section of the trail or complete the entire route, the GR20 promises an immersive and unforgettable hiking experience in the heart of Corsica's untamed wilderness.
