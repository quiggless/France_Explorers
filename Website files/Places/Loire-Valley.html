Discover all that Loire Valley has to offer!

What is there to do in Loire Valley?

Loire Valley, often referred to as the "Garden of France," is a region known for its majestic châteaux, historic vineyards, and scenic landscapes. Nestled along the Loire River, this enchanting area is a treasure trove of cultural heritage and natural beauty. Explore the diverse attractions that Loire Valley has to offer, from grand castles to charming villages, and immerse yourself in the rich history and picturesque surroundings that define this UNESCO World Heritage site.

  • Visit Château de Chambord

    Embark on a journey through time as you visit the iconic Château de Chambord. This Renaissance masterpiece is renowned for its grandeur and architectural splendor. Explore the intricate details of the castle, including its distinctive double-helix staircase attributed to Leonardo da Vinci. Stroll through the vast park surrounding the château and marvel at the blending of French medieval and classical Italian architecture. Château de Chambord stands as a testament to the opulence of the French Renaissance and offers a captivating glimpse into the region's regal history.

  • Explore the Gardens of Villandry

    Immerse yourself in the enchanting beauty of the Gardens of Villandry. This exceptional Renaissance garden, located near the Château de Villandry, captivates visitors with its meticulously designed ornamental flower beds, water features, and geometric patterns. Take a leisurely stroll through the various themed gardens, including the famous Knot Garden and the Water Garden. The Gardens of Villandry offer a harmonious blend of horticulture and artistry, making it a must-visit destination for lovers of nature and garden design.

  • Discover Château de Chenonceau

    Step into the elegant world of Château de Chenonceau, often referred to as the "Ladies' Castle." This iconic château spans the Cher River and boasts a unique blend of Gothic and Renaissance architecture. Explore the opulent rooms adorned with period furniture, tapestries, and art collections. Stroll through the beautiful gardens that surround the château and admire the breathtaking reflections in the river. Château de Chenonceau's romantic charm and historical significance make it one of the most visited and cherished castles in the Loire Valley.

  • Wine Tasting in Sancerre

    Indulge in the exquisite wines of the Loire Valley with a visit to the charming town of Sancerre. This renowned wine region is celebrated for its crisp and aromatic Sauvignon Blanc wines. Explore local vineyards, meet passionate winemakers, and savor the flavors of the region. The picturesque landscapes of Sancerre, overlooking the Loire River, provide a perfect backdrop for a relaxing wine-tasting experience. Whether you're a seasoned wine enthusiast or a casual connoisseur, Sancerre offers a delightful journey through the vineyards of the Loire Valley.

  • Explore the Caves of Rochemenier

    Delve into the fascinating underground world of the Caves of Rochemenier. This unique troglodyte site takes visitors on a journey through an ancient village carved into the soft tuffeau limestone. Explore the interconnected rooms, including dwellings, stables, and even a chapel, all hewn from the rock. The Caves of Rochemenier offer a captivating glimpse into the ingenuity of past generations who adapted to the natural resources of the Loire Valley. Discover the charm and mystery of this underground village, where history and geology converge in a truly extraordinary setting.
