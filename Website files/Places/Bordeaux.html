What is there to do in Bordeaux?

Whether you're on a road trip or a city break, Bordeaux is an incredible French city that seamlessly blends history with modern allure. Nestled in Southwestern France, it serves as the capital of the Nouvelle Aquitaine region, boasting an alluring ambiance and a tapestry of cultural experiences. Recognized for its well-preserved architecture such as Palace De La Bourse, Bordeaux is undeniably one of France's most beautiful cities, featuring charming cobblestone streets and grandiose buildings.


  • Explore Bordeaux's Historic Center:

    Immerse yourself in the enchanting ambiance of Bordeaux's historic city center, often referred to as the "Port of the Moon." A UNESCO World Heritage site, this area captivates visitors with its meticulously preserved architecture, including medieval churches and elegant 18th-century mansions. Wander through lively squares like Place de la Victoire and lose yourself in the narrow, winding streets that reveal hidden gems at every turn.


  • Visit Palace de la Bourse:

    Experience Bordeaux's most iconic square by visiting Palace de la Bourse. This monument was constructed in the 18th century and is a recognized on the UNESCO World Heritage List as "an outstanding urban and architectural ensemble." The building was originally designed by architect "Ange-Jacques Gabriel" and was created so merchants could conduct their business however is now being used as a National Customs museum as of 1984.

Whether exploring the prestigious châteaux of Saint-Émilion or meandering through the vine-covered landscapes of the Médoc, indulge in wine tastings that showcase the region's diverse terroirs and winemaking traditions.


  • Visit the Darwin Ecosystem:

    Uncover the avant-garde spirit of Bordeaux at the Darwin Ecosystem, a unique space fostering creativity, entrepreneurship, and urban agriculture. Here, street art blends seamlessly with sustainable initiatives, creating a dynamic environment that reflects Bordeaux's commitment to innovation and community engagement.


  • Take a River Cruise:

    Enhance your Bordeaux experience with a leisurely river cruise along the Garonne. Drift along the historic riverbanks, passing under iconic bridges and witnessing the city's landmarks from a fresh perspective. Whether during the day or under the city lights at night, a river cruise provides a captivating view of Bordeaux's architectural wonders.
